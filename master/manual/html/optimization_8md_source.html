1 Optimization {#cha-optimization}
2 ============
3 
4 While sampling from a posterior with Markov chains, BAT will store the
5 maximum value of the posterior that it has seen (and the corresponing
6 point in parameter space). But the sampling algorithm is designed to
7 sample, not to find a maximum value. To find the maximum point, BAT
8 includes optimization methods. These are called via
9 `BCIntegrate::FindMode`, which takes optional arguments: You may give
10 it a starting point; if you do not specify one, BAT will start from
11 the current maximum point. You can also specify the optimization
12 method when you call the function; or you can specify it in advance
13 via `BCIntegrate::SetOptimzationMethod`.
14 
15 By default, BAT replaces its currently held maximum point only if the
16 newly found one has a greater posterior. You can tell BAT to replace
17 the currently held one regardless of whether optimization improves
18 upon the currently stored one via the function
19 `BCIntegrate::SetFlagIgnorePrevOptimization`.
20 
21 # Minuit {#sec-minuit}
22 
23 BAT uses Minuit through ROOT's interface to it. To use it, set the
24 optimization method to `BCIntegrate::kOptMinuit`.
25 
26 Minuit is what is known as a gradient follower---it moves from a
27 starting point in the direction that increases the posterior until it
28 finds a maximum. Minuit is much better at finding the exact location
29 of a maximum than sampling with Markov chains is, but it does not
30 gaurantee that this maximum is the global maximum or only a local
31 one. Sampling with Markov chains can better identify the region of the
32 global maximum than Minuit can. So we recommend that you first
33 marginalize your model and then call `FindMode`.
34 
35 # Simulated Annealing {#sec-simulated-annealing}
36 
37 BAT provides a simulated annealing algorithm for optimization. To use
38 it, set the optimization method to `BCIntegrate::kOptSimAnn`.
39 
40 This algorithm is similar to the Metropolis-Hastings sampling one in
41 that involves proposal of new points to move to randomly in a
42 neighborhood of the current point. But the neighborhood and the
43 acceptance criteria are regulated in such a way as to encourage motion
44 towards the global maximum.
45 
46 Using BAT, simulated annealing will in general not find the maximum
47 point as precisely or rapidly as Minuit, but it can more reliably find
48 the global maximum instead of a local maximum. We therefore recommend
49 calling `FindMode` with `BCIntegrate::kOptMinuit` after calling it
50 with `kOptSimAnn`. This is similar to first sampling and then
51 optimizing with Minuit; but simulated annealing is less
52 calculationally expensive than sampling---that is, it will call your
53 likelihood less often. However, simulated annealing is not a sampling
54 technique and will not provide output samples for further offline use.
55 
56 There are several parameters that govern the running of simulated
57 annealing. Please consult the code documentation and C. Brachem's
58 _Implementation and test of a simulated annealing algorithm in the
59 Bayesian Analysis Tookit_ (2009) for how to set them. The most
60 important option you may change is the proposal function. This is set
61 via `BCIntegrate::SetSASchedule`. The options are
62 `BCIntegrate::kSACauchy`, `BCIntegrate::kSABoltzmann`, and
63 `BCIntegrate::kSACustom`. (The latter requires you to set your own
64 proposal function.)
